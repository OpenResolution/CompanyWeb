TurboSMLM



To generate super-resolution images, Single-molecule localization microscope (SMLM) requires intensive computation process. Current solutions, including commercialized products, mandate a two-step process: first, the acquisition of the dataset, followed by its processing. This sequential approach means users cannot visualize the image in real-time during acquisition, leaving them in the dark about their sample until post-processing.

Recognizing this issue, we reprogrammed the state-of-the-art SMLM reconstruction algorithm in Taichi-a high-performance parallel computation framework, accelerating the reconstruction speed hundreds of times faster than the serial processing CPU approach. We name this program TurboSMLM. Given TurboSMLM’s superior reconstruction speed surpasses the image acquisition speed, we can process data during acquisition, providing a near real-time image reconstruction on SMLM system. Users could access the super-resolution image while taking images.
