Deep learning adaptive optics (DLAO)



Deep learning adaptive optics (DLAO) is a deep neuron network algorithm that interactively controls the deformable mirror to compensate for the aberration in the imaging system from the feedback generated by the image taken.

Emission patterns from individual fluorescence molecules offer insights into their molecular center positions and shared wavefront distortion. However, the unpredictable positions of blinking fluorescent molecules and their limited emitted photons in SMLM experiments complicate direct wavefront measurements. DLAO, a single-molecule deep neural network, has shown to deduce wavefront distortions from individual PSFs 28 aberration modes almost instantly during SMLM imaging amidst intricate wavefront distortions, such as those caused by refractive index disparities. This system's capability to compensate for numerous aberrations also underlines the DLAO's autonomous mirror control capacity against random and evolving aberration shifts.
