SMLM

Single-molecule localization microscope (SMLM) is a type of super-resolution microscope including Stochastic Optical Reconstruction Microscopy (STORM), Photoactivated Localization Microscopy (PALM), and DNA points accumulation for imaging in nanoscale topography (DNA-PAINT). The principle of SMLM is based on isolating the fluorescence emission from individual molecules, even if they are closely packed together. In a typical SMLM experiment, only a sparse subset of fluorescent molecules is activated to the fluorescent state, ensuring that activated molecules are sufficiently far apart from each other. This allows for the precise localization of each activated molecule. Repeating this process thousands of times, with different subsets of molecules activated in each cycle, a high-resolution image is reconstructed from the accumulated localizations.
